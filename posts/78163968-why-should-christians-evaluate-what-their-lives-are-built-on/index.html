    Why Should Christians Evaluate What Their Lives Are Built On?

    March 7, 2024 · 2 min
    Table of Contents

    • Examining Our Spiritual Foundation Regularly
    • The Role of Scripture in Our Lives
    • Conclusion

    Christians are called to evaluate what their lives are built on in order to ensure that their foundation is firmly established on the biblical principles and teachings of Jesus Christ. This process of examination helps to build a strong spiritual foundation, identifying areas where improvements may be needed, and ensuring we are walking in accordance with God's will for our lives.

    Building on the Foundation of Christ

    The Bible presents Jesus Christ as the cornerstone upon which believers should construct their lives (1 Corinthians 3:11; Ephesians 2:20-22). As Christ-followers, we must make sure that everything in our life is in alignment with His teachings. This includes our morals, values, action, relationships, beliefs, and even priorities.

    Examining Our Spiritual Foundation Regularly

    Regular evaluation of our spiritual foundation allows us to identify areas where growth or transformation is needed, particularly as seasons of life change. As time passes, circumstances in our lives and the world around us can challenge our convictions, convincing us to deviate from Christ's teachings. Regular examination prevents gradual erosion or compromise.

Are grapes gathered from thornbushes, or figs from thistles? 17 So, every healthy tree bears good fruit, but the diseased tree bears bad fruit. 18 A healthy tree cannot bear bad fruit, nor can a diseased tree bear good fruit. 19 Every tree that does not bear good fruit is cut down and thrown into the fire. 20 Thus you will recognize them by their fruits. Matthew 7:15-20

    The Role of Scripture in Our Lives

    God's Word serves as an anchor to keep us grounded in truth. Regular reading and study of Scripture help us discern between right and wrong, keep us connected with Christ, and enable us to apply biblical principles in our lives. The evaluation process encourages us to align our priorities with God's plans and purpose, nurturing spiritual maturity.

    Conclusion

    For Christians to grow spiritually strong and avoid drifting away from God's design for their lives, they need to evaluate what their lives are built on consistently. By examining their spiritual foundation against the standard of Scripture and Christ's teachings, Christians can identify areas for improvement, affirming the healthiness and stability of their relationship with God.
